Bye Superman!

July 20, 2025

A fictional character first came to life in 1938 on the pages of Action Comics #1: an archetype who wears an outlandish costume, has a codename, extraordinary abilities, and an altruistic mission. He's a starman, sent here to preserve humanity. While that sounds like utter bullshit propaganda spread to mollify the general populance, I have to admit it's true. Because he's better than a saviour – he's a human. Not more alien than me or you? Why, you ask? Because he cares, he loves, and he attends to his ablutions (LOL!). That's what makes him special, what makes him ours, what makes him incredibly easy to love. He's Superman! The best of all of us, the best of all humanity can offer. Anyone can be Superman because we are already a part of him; our love, passion, hate, fears, and idiosyncrasies make this 'man' 'super' – it's a matter of attention and empathy. My Superman walked in with a hiking bag and spectacles on. A muscular bloke with an ample frame. His eyes were and still are full of hope, so much so that it's hard to face them (with my degrading-self). I would not like to romanticize this weirdo anymore, because he's pretty good at that himself – someone might even say "over-romanticizing" himself. Well, I'm here to announce that I'm letting my Superman go; I'm letting him go for good. I don't know what the world holds for me or him, but all I have is this image of my bruv, sparkling like a star in a crowd of millions. And some amount of courage is bestowed by him. I'm scared and I'm sad about losing him, but that's what makes me human, and that's what makes us Superman! To Mr. CEO, At last we'll meet someday. Hopefully then I'll be able to catch the unbounded, infinite love and appreciation you have for yours truly.
